Land Subsidence

As one who come from Shanghai, which is a low-lying coastal city, I attach great importance to this issue.
Land subsidence occurs when the ground sinks or settles due to various factors, including over-extraction of groundwater, mining activities, and geological processes. This can lead to the flooding of coastal areas, increased vulnerability to storm surges, and damage to infrastructure.

Climate Change

The release of greenhouse gases, primarily carbon dioxide, through activities like burning fossil fuels and deforestation, contributes to global warming. This leads to rising temperatures, altered weather patterns, and sea-level rise.

Loss of Biodiversity

Environmental degradation, such as habitat destruction and pollution, can lead to a loss of biodiversity. This can disrupt ecosystems and reduce the resilience of natural systems to adapt to changes.

Food Security Issues

Soil degradation, water pollution, and climate change can lead to reduced agricultural productivity, potentially resulting in food shortages and increased food prices.
